# Product Vision

## Summary

**Philosophies of a Cyborg** is Logarn's public thinking engine: part blog, part
field notebook, part intellectual archive. It should make serious ideas feel
sharp and personal without becoming academic wallpaper or startup thought-leader
porridge.

The site should establish a recognizable voice quickly, then reward depth. A new
visitor should understand within seconds that this is a distinctive personal
publication. A returning reader should have strong tools for following threads,
finding older work, and reading deeply.

## Audience

- Readers interested in AI, agency, software, philosophy, productivity,
  automation, and the weird edge where humans start outsourcing pieces of
  themselves to machines.
- Friends, collaborators, clients, and internet strangers who need a fast way to
  understand Logarn's taste, thinking, and standards.
- Future Logarn, using the archive as memory, portfolio, and idea warehouse.

## Positioning

The site is not:

- A generic portfolio.
- A SaaS landing page.
- A newsletter squeeze page.
- A pile of markdown in public with prettier fonts.

The site is:

- A curated personal publication.
- A long-term archive for ideas and experiments.
- A place where essays, notes, links, and series can coexist without turning into
  a junk drawer with CSS.

## Core Content Types

- **Essays:** polished, durable pieces with strong typography, table of contents,
  footnotes, references, and related reading.
- **Notes:** shorter observations, fragments, logs, and unfinished thinking.
- **Links:** annotated external links with commentary and tags.
- **Series:** ordered collections around a sustained topic.
- **Experiments:** optional interactive/code-heavy pieces that may need custom
  layouts later.

## Success Criteria

- A reader can find and finish a long essay comfortably on desktop and mobile.
- A reader can browse by topic, series, and date without getting lost.
- Logarn can publish a new note or essay without touching app code.
- The visual system feels custom and memorable while staying quiet around prose.
- Pages pass baseline performance, accessibility, and metadata checks.

## Non-Goals For V1

- Comments.
- User accounts.
- Paywalls.
- Full CMS admin UI.
- AI chat over the archive.
- Newsletter sending infrastructure.
- Heavy animated spectacle on every page.

Those can come later. V1 earns trust by making the writing experience excellent.
